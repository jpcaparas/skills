# Content and Information Architecture

Use this file for template-level content quality, duplication, page targeting, heading structure, internal linking, and topical coverage.

## Page-Type Review

For every index-worthy page type, ask:

- What search intent is this page supposed to satisfy?
- Is the visible copy specific to that intent, or generic template filler?
- Is the primary heading distinct and aligned with the title?
- Does the page contain enough unique substance to deserve indexation?

## Duplication Patterns

Look for duplication caused by:

- near-identical programmatic pages
- faceted or parameterized variants
- location or service pages with only token word swaps
- category pages and filtered subviews competing for the same terms
- article templates reusing stock intros, FAQs, and CTAs with minimal differentiation

When duplication is structural, the fix belongs in routing, canonicalization, indexing rules, or template logic, not just copy edits.

## Internal Linking

Check:

- whether important pages receive contextual links from relevant pages
- whether anchor text reflects destination topic instead of “learn more”
- whether breadcrumbs reinforce hierarchy
- whether hub pages connect clusters of related content
- whether new content is orphaned after publication

## Heading and Layout Signals

Check:

- one clear primary heading per page
- section headings that organize the page meaningfully
- no layout bug that duplicates H1s or injects empty headings
- supporting media and tables that improve scannability rather than bury meaning

## Content Quality Heuristics

Useful heuristics:

- clear answer or value near the top
- evidence of first-hand expertise or specificity where the topic demands it
- obvious ownership, authorship, or business identity when relevant
- distinct value compared with template siblings
- current and maintained information on pages where freshness matters

Do not reduce this to word count. The problem is usually low information gain, not short length by itself.
