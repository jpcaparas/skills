# Gotchas

## 1. Clarity can turn into shrink-wrap

Symptom:

- the draft is easier to parse but no longer sounds like finished writing

Cause:

- every sentence was shortened
- every bridge was removed

Fix:

- return to `references/voice-and-rhythm.md`
- reconnect related sentences
- restore contrast, consequence, and one or two natural asides

## 2. Style cannot rescue the wrong shape

Symptom:

- the prose sounds sharper sentence by sentence, but the whole piece still feels wrong

Cause:

- the draft is a memo wearing essay sentences, or a guide wearing article sentences

Fix:

- choose the right mode in `references/genre-modes.md`
- rebuild the opening and ending before polishing again

## 3. Empty force words feel like progress

Symptom:

- the draft sounds more dramatic after editing but not more persuasive

Cause:

- abstractions and importance markers replaced evidence

Fix:

- use `references/genericity-and-stiffness.md`
- swap praise words for facts, numbers, actors, or consequences

## 4. Borrowed voice becomes imitation

Symptom:

- the draft sounds like a diluted version of someone else

Cause:

- the edit copied surface tics rather than operating habits

Fix:

- pick one primary bundle in `references/style-bundles.md`
- borrow structure, emphasis, and evidence discipline only

## 5. Openings absorb too much anxiety

Symptom:

- the first paragraph tries to explain the whole piece

Cause:

- the writer wants to front-load every justification

Fix:

- write the smallest honest opening that earns the next paragraph
- let the page do the rest of the work later

## 6. Endings recap because the middle is doing too little

Symptom:

- the conclusion sounds tidy but unnecessary

Cause:

- the article waited until the end to say what it thinks

Fix:

- move the real point earlier
- let the ending add consequence, image, or next step instead of summary

## 7. Bullets can hide missing thinking

Symptom:

- the page looks cleaner after turning paragraphs into bullets, but meaning did not improve

Cause:

- list formatting substituted for argument or sequence

Fix:

- use bullets only when they genuinely compress steps, tradeoffs, or evidence

## 8. No stop condition means over-editing

Symptom:

- each pass changes the draft but does not clearly improve it

Cause:

- the edit has no completion standard

Fix:

- stop when the reader can follow the sentence on first read, the paragraph has one job, the opening earns the rest, and the ending adds something new
