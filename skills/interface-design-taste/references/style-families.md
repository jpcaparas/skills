# Style Families

Pick one family as the primary lane. Borrowing accents is fine. Mixing whole families usually creates a confused interface.

## Family Table

| Family | Best For | Typography | Surfaces | Motion |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Editorial Calm | content-rich products, docs, thoughtful SaaS | refined sans + restrained serif accents | quiet borders, almost-flat surfaces | subtle, slow, mostly fade and shift |
| Product Precision | B2B tools, dashboards, fintech, enterprise | sharp sans + mono for numbers | crisp dividers, disciplined cards, low blur | short, informative, state-led |
| Warm Material | premium consumer products, wellness, hospitality, creative tools | humanist sans, warmer display accents | layered but soft, tactile edges | fluid and welcoming, never frantic |
| Operational Density | admin panels, ops consoles, desktop work tools | compact sans + strong mono support | dividers over cards, minimal ornament | restrained, utilitarian, fast |
| Expressive Showcase | launches, portfolios, campaigns, reveal pages | bold display type with strong contrast | fewer surfaces, bigger visual gestures | high-energy narrative motion |

## Editorial Calm

Use when the interface should feel considered, articulate, and slightly literary without becoming nostalgic.

- Priorities:
  - clean reading measure
  - strong whitespace rhythm
  - quiet neutrals with one muted accent
- Avoid:
  - overly dense card mosaics
  - neon or saturated accents
  - overly animated hero sections

Suggested axes:

- composure: high
- density: low to medium
- contrast: medium
- materiality: low
- motion: low

## Product Precision

Use when trust, scanability, and operational clarity matter more than mood performance.

- Priorities:
  - clear table and form behavior
  - strong type hierarchy without dramatic display moments
  - repeated spacing and border logic
- Avoid:
  - soft luxury treatments on hard-working tools
  - decorative gradients as the main identity
  - random component radii and shadow styles

Suggested axes:

- composure: medium to high
- density: medium to high
- contrast: high
- materiality: low to medium
- motion: low to medium

## Warm Material

Use when the product should feel humane, premium, and tactile rather than clinical.

- Priorities:
  - warm neutrals
  - gentle depth
  - friendly but precise typography
- Avoid:
  - sugary palettes
  - oversoft shadows on every object
  - slow decorative motion in dense workflows

Suggested axes:

- composure: medium
- density: low to medium
- contrast: soft to medium
- materiality: medium to high
- motion: medium

## Operational Density

Use when speed, oversight, and information compression matter. This is especially strong for desktop software.

- Priorities:
  - strong grouping without card overuse
  - dense but regular rhythm
  - compact controls with clear affordances
- Avoid:
  - marketing-page whitespace
  - giant hero typography
  - oversized radius and glass effects

Suggested axes:

- composure: high
- density: high
- contrast: high
- materiality: low
- motion: low

## Expressive Showcase

Use when the job is persuasion, storytelling, or memorability, not long-session operation.

- Priorities:
  - one dominant visual idea per section
  - bolder asymmetry
  - larger motion contrasts and richer image treatment
- Avoid:
  - importing this whole family into core product surfaces
  - piling multiple special effects into one view
  - making text work harder than it needs to

Suggested axes:

- composure: low to medium
- density: low
- contrast: medium to high
- materiality: medium
- motion: medium to high

## Choosing Between Families

Use this shortcut:

- if the product is read-heavy, start with Editorial Calm
- if the product is work-heavy, start with Product Precision
- if the product is care-oriented or sensorial, start with Warm Material
- if the product is dense and tool-like, start with Operational Density
- if the product is promotional or reveal-driven, start with Expressive Showcase
